The System

A turnkey education campaign system, not an ad package.

Hero HomeReach is not selling lender ad management or a generic Facebook campaign. The campaign system is built around buyer education first: a targeted Hero audience, a dedicated Hero HomeReach campaign page, a guide or tool, follow-up education and a local lender follow-up path.

Meta may help introduce the buyer to the path, but the value is the education system behind the click.

How Inquiries Happen

Buyer inquiries generated through education.

Hero HomeReach campaigns are designed to meet buyers before they are ready to start with a loan application.

A buyer may enter through a VA readiness guide, a CHFA explainer, a MetroDPA question, a seller concession resource or a homebuyer education topic.

When that buyer requests help through Hero HomeReach, the selected lender partner receives the inquiry and follows up as the local education-aligned contact.

The first conversation can begin with context:

  • What the buyer requested
  • What topic they were exploring
  • What questions they may have
  • What next step they asked for

This is not a cold list of names. It is an education-generated buyer inquiry.

The Difference

The conversation starts with the buyer’s question.

Most mortgage marketing starts with a lender asking for attention. Hero HomeReach starts with the buyer’s question.

  • Can I afford to start?
  • What does VA zero down really cover?
  • How does CHFA actually work?
  • Is MetroDPA repayable?
  • Can seller concessions change the cash-to-close plan?
  • What does homebuyer education require?

The goal is not to rush the buyer into an application. The goal is to help them understand the path clearly enough to take the next responsible step.

Partner Fit

Partner fit matters because buyer trust is the asset.

Hero HomeReach is not designed for every lender. The platform is built around education-first buyers who may be trying to understand VA loan planning, CHFA, MetroDPA, seller concessions, homebuyer education, credit readiness or local assistance options.

That means lender partners need more than product knowledge. They need the patience and clarity to guide buyers without pressure, overpromising or turning an education path into a sales sprint.

We are looking for partners who can protect the trust that education creates.

This may be a fit if your lending team…
  • Understands Colorado assistance programs or is committed to learning them deeply
  • Can explain CHFA, MetroDPA, VA loan planning, seller concessions and homebuyer education in plain English
  • Knows how to guide early-stage buyers without pushing them before they are ready
  • Values long-term trust over short-term pressure
  • Can handle buyer conversations with patience, accuracy and follow-through
  • Wants to be associated with an education-first Colorado homebuyer platform
This is probably not the right fit if you are looking for…
  • A generic Facebook ad package
  • A guaranteed lead pipeline
  • A list of buyers to call immediately
  • A campaign that skips education and goes straight to application pressure
  • Messaging built around hype, urgency or unsupported promises
  • A volume-first lead source with no concern for buyer experience

Hero HomeReach is built for lenders who want education-generated buyer inquiries and better first conversations, not just more names in a spreadsheet.
