How the System Works

The path from buyer question to lender conversation.

Hero HomeReach campaigns are not random ads pointed at a generic form. Each path connects a specific Hero audience to education, guide delivery, follow-up and a local lender conversation with context.

This is not a list of names from a generic ad campaign. It is an education path.
The buyer sees a relevant message, lands on a Hero HomeReach page, receives a helpful guide, gets follow-up education and then, when they request help, becomes an education-generated buyer inquiry for the selected lender partner.
Step 1

Targeted Hero audience campaigns

Education-first Meta campaigns introduce Colorado veterans, educators, first responders and other Hero audiences to a path built around their real homebuyer questions.

Step 2

Dedicated Hero HomeReach campaign pages

Each campaign points buyers to a focused education page, not a generic contact form. The page matches the audience, the question and the next helpful resource.

Step 3

Guide delivery and resource access

When a buyer raises their hand, they receive useful education immediately: guides, tools and plain-English next steps that help them understand the path.

Step 4

Education-first follow-up

Follow-up keeps teaching instead of pressuring. Buyers receive helpful education around programs, planning, cash-to-close and when a local conversation may make sense.

Step 5

Buyer inquiry context

When the lender partner follows up, the conversation can begin with context: what the buyer requested, what topic they explored and what question brought them in.

The Difference

The conversation starts with the buyer’s question.

Most mortgage marketing starts with a lender asking for attention. Hero HomeReach starts with the buyer’s question.

  • Can I afford to start?
  • What does VA zero down really cover?
  • How does CHFA actually work?
  • Is MetroDPA repayable?
  • Can seller concessions change the cash-to-close plan?
  • What does homebuyer education require?

The goal is not to rush the buyer into an application. The goal is to help them understand the path clearly enough to take the next responsible step.

Partner Fit

Partner fit matters because buyer trust is the asset.

Hero HomeReach works best with lender partners who can match the pace and tone of an education-first buyer — someone still learning VA loan planning, CHFA, MetroDPA, seller concessions or credit readiness, not yet ready for a sales conversation.

That means lender partners need more than product knowledge. They need the patience and clarity to guide buyers without pressure, overpromising or turning an education path into a sales sprint.

We are looking for partners who can protect the trust that education creates.

This may be a fit if your lending team…
  • Understands Colorado assistance programs or is committed to learning them deeply
  • Can explain CHFA, MetroDPA, VA loan planning, seller concessions and homebuyer education in plain English
  • Knows how to guide early-stage buyers without pushing them before they are ready
  • Values long-term trust over short-term pressure
  • Can handle buyer conversations with patience, accuracy and follow-through
  • Wants to be associated with an education-first Colorado homebuyer platform
This is probably not the right fit if you are looking for…
  • A generic Facebook ad package
  • A guaranteed lead pipeline
  • A list of buyers to call immediately
  • A campaign that skips education and goes straight to application pressure
  • Messaging built around hype, urgency or unsupported promises
  • A volume-first lead source with no concern for buyer experience

Hero HomeReach is built for lenders who want education-generated buyer inquiries and better first conversations, not just more names in a spreadsheet.

Secondary Benefit

A stronger reason to start agent conversations.

Most lender-agent conversations begin with a familiar ask: send me buyers.

Hero HomeReach gives selected lender partners a different starting point.

Instead of leading with a rate sheet or a generic lending pitch, a partner can point to education-first campaigns built around the questions Colorado buyers are already asking: VA cash-to-close, CHFA, MetroDPA, seller concessions, homebuyer education and first-time buyer readiness.

When those campaigns generate buyer inquiries, the lender has something more useful to discuss with real estate partners: what buyers are asking, where they are confused and how a better-prepared buyer can move through the process with more confidence.

This is not the main reason Hero HomeReach exists. Buyer education comes first.

But for the right lender, the education path can also become a better business-development asset: not a pitch for referrals, but a way to bring useful buyer insight into agent relationships.
